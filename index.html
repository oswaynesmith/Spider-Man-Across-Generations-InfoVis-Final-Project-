The Maguire Era

2002 – 2007

Directed by Sam Raimi, the original trilogy defined superhero cinema for a generation. Spider-Man 2 remains one of the most critically lauded comic book films ever made — winning the Academy Award for Best Visual Effects. The era peaked early and stumbled with a polarizing third entry.

The Garfield Era

2012 – 2014

Sony's reboot brought fresh energy with Andrew Garfield's charming, quippy Peter Parker and a new love interest in Gwen Stacy. Critics were cautiously optimistic about the first film — but the franchise struggled to find its footing. The Amazing Spider-Man 2 became the lowest-rated and lowest-grossing entry in the franchise, ending the series prematurely.

The Holland Era

2017 – 2019

Tom Holland's Peter Parker debuted in Captain America: Civil War before getting his own MCU trilogy. With the weight of Marvel's interconnected universe behind him — and Tony Stark as a mentor — this era delivered consistently high critic scores and growing box office returns, setting the stage for something unprecedented.

The Verdict

Across two decades, the Spider-Man franchise has continually reinvented itself. Here's what the data tells us.

🕷

Critics' Darling

The Maguire era holds the highest average critic scores. Spider-Man 2 remains the gold standard with 93% on Rotten Tomatoes and a Metascore of 83, still unmatched by any other entry.

📉

The Lost Era

The Garfield era scored lowest across every metric. TASM2's 51% RT score and $203M gross which is the franchise's lowest and made a third film commercially untenable and led directly to the MCU partnership.

💰

Box Office Supremacy

The MCU era dominates at the box office. No Way Home's $814M domestic gross is nearly double Spider-Man (2002)'s $408M, and that's without counting the MCU films it was embedded within.
